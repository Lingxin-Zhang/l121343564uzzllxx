# Round 33 Summary

## Scope

Round 33 only revisits Fig. 3 under the fixed cache-selected block width
`w=14`. It measures `PackedBlockLUTKernel.apply_many_packed` versus
`PackedBatchGF2Kernel.apply_many` for BCH(255,239), r16. No Fig. 2, Fig. 4,
OFEC, decoder, or BER files were modified.

Tasks:

- syndrome: `v H^T`
- parity: `u P`

Every timed throughput row first checks bit-exact agreement against
`NaiveGF2Kernel`.

## Batch-1000 Diagnosis

The measured data does not support the stronger interpretation that
`batch ~= 1000` is a proven L1-fit peak.

Evidence:

- In the 10-round bulk data, syndrome best LUT throughput is at batch `1500`,
  and the best ratio is at batch `2000`.
- In the 10-round bulk data, parity best LUT throughput and best ratio are both
  at batch `2000`.
- Batch `1000` is near a nominal 32 KiB active-input footprint, but batch
  `1500` and `2000` exceed that footprint and are as fast or faster.
- Stage timing remains index-dominated. Around batch `700` to `3000`, the
  read/prepare and XOR percentages change smoothly, not as a sharp L1 boundary.
- The all-round scatter plot shows visible timing noise, especially after
  batch `3000`, but the small/mid-batch plateau shape is consistent across
  rounds.

Conservative diagnosis: the current data supports a small/mid-batch plateau
with later degradation, not a narrow batch-1000 L1-fit peak. The batch-1000
point is useful as a fixed-chunk streaming operating point, but not as proof of
the proposed L1-residency mechanism.
